Dav Clark is appointed as a data scientist at the Berkeley D-Lab, a Berkeley Institute for Data Science Fellow, and a Lecturer in the School of Information Masters in Data Science. He is also a co-investigator for an NIMH-funded trial at Kennedy Krieger Institute evaluating the impacts of taiji on adolescents with ADHD. His primary focus is the use of lightweight social measurement using mobile and web technologies to assess behavior change interventions. His research is conducted under the umbrella of Glass Bead Labs.

Sid Feygin is a PhD student in Civil Systems at UC Berkeley working in the Smart Cities Research Center. He is interested in developing an integrative approach to data collection and citizen feedback at the city scale. He has helped develop and run large mobile research efforts in collaboration with XLab at Haas as part of XMobile using the ODK toolkit. In addition, he will be investigating the impact of affective state on decision-making with researchers from Economics and Computer Science.

Tchiki Davis is a Ph.D. student in the area of social/personality psychology at UC Berkeley and an NIMH “translational research and intervention development” research fellow. She has developed an arsenal of survey, physiological, behavioral, and qualitative research skills in an effort to 1.) better understand people’s emotions, health, and well-being, and 2.) develop research based products, tools, apps, and interventions that improve health and wellness.
